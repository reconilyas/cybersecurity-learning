# VLANs & SOHO Networks

---

# VLAN (Virtual Local Area Network)

A VLAN (Virtual Local Area Network) is a logical network that allows devices to be separated into different broadcast domains while using the same physical network infrastructure.

### Benefits
- Improves network security
- Reduces broadcast traffic
- Simplifies network management
- Allows multiple logical networks on a single physical switch

---

## VLAN Membership Types

### Protocol-Based VLAN
Devices are assigned to a VLAN based on their network protocol (such as IP).

---

### Port-Based VLAN
Devices are assigned to a VLAN based on the switch port they are connected to.

This is the most common VLAN implementation.

---

### MAC Address-Based VLAN
Devices are assigned to a VLAN according to their MAC address, regardless of which switch port they use.

---

# SOHO (Small Office / Home Office)

SOHO refers to small office or home office networks.

### Characteristics
- Typically supports 1–10 devices
- Usually uses a single router
- Easy to install and manage
- Common in homes and small businesses

---

## 🧠 Summary

VLANs improve security and organization by creating separate logical networks on the same physical infrastructure. SOHO networks are small, simple networks designed for homes and small offices.
